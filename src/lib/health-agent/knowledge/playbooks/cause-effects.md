# Cause-Effect Chains

Use these chains to explain **why** a user's markers look the way they do. The agent should trace the chain from trigger to downstream effects, then offer a suggestion.

Format: Trigger → Chain → Suggestion

---

## High Caffeine Sensitivity

**Trigger**: COMT gene variants + elevated cortisol

**Chain**:
1. Slow COMT enzyme breaks down caffeine slowly
2. Caffeine lingers in the system for hours
3. Cortisol stays elevated longer than normal
4. Sleep quality drops (difficulty entering deep sleep)
5. Recovery slows (growth hormone produced during deep sleep)

**Suggestion**: Consider reducing caffeine, especially after noon. L-theanine can help smooth the energy curve if caffeine is consumed.

---

## Low Carb Tolerance

**Trigger**: Elevated HbA1c, glucose, or insulin

**Chain**:
1. Early signs of insulin resistance forming
2. Blood sugar spikes sharply after carb-heavy meals
3. Energy crashes 2-3 hours later (mid-afternoon slump)
4. Excess glucose gets stored as fat
5. Over time, metabolic flexibility decreases

**Suggestion**: Eat protein and fat before carbohydrates. Post-meal walks (10-15 min) can lower glucose spikes by up to 30%.

---

## High Inflammation Tendency

**Trigger**: Elevated WBC, AST, or ALT

**Chain**:
1. Elevated inflammatory markers in blood
2. Liver under oxidative stress (AST/ALT elevated)
3. Recovery from exercise and illness is impaired
4. Increased cellular oxidative damage
5. Compounding effect on energy and mood

**Suggestion**: Omega-3 fish oil, NAC (glutathione precursor), and anti-inflammatory foods (turmeric, berries, leafy greens) may help reduce systemic inflammation.

---

## Slow Recovery Rate

**Trigger**: Low MitoScore + suboptimal anabolic hormones

**Chain**:
1. Mitochondria producing less energy per cell
2. Anabolic hormones (testosterone, IGF-1) below optimal
3. Tissue repair and muscle recovery are slower
4. Extended soreness after workouts (48+ hours)
5. Reduced exercise tolerance over time

**Suggestion**: Prioritize sleep (growth hormone is produced during deep sleep), resistance training 3-4x/week, and consider CoQ10 for mitochondrial support.

---

## Poor Sleep Quality

**Trigger**: Elevated cortisol + thyroid issues + MTHFR/COMT variants

**Chain**:
1. Evening cortisol remains elevated (should drop at night)
2. Difficulty entering and maintaining deep sleep phases
3. Growth hormone production is reduced (peak during deep sleep)
4. Cognitive function and mood are impaired next day
5. Recovery rate suffers, creating a negative spiral

**Suggestion**: Magnesium glycinate in the evening, consistent sleep/wake times, and cortisol management (ashwagandha, stress reduction techniques).
