# Sleep Quality

Your genetic and hormonal predisposition for sleep quality. High cortisol, thyroid issues, and certain gene variants can disrupt sleep architecture.

## Derivation Rules

- **Poor**: Cortisol above optimal AND (thyroid sub-optimal OR MTHFR/COMT gene variants present)
- **Average**: Cortisol near upper boundary OR thyroid borderline
- **Good**: Cortisol within optimal AND thyroid optimal AND low genetic risk

## What This Means for the User

When sleep quality tendency is **poor**, the user may experience:
- Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
- Waking between 2-4 AM (cortisol spike)
- Feeling unrefreshed despite 7-8 hours in bed
- Daytime fatigue, brain fog, and irritability
- Reduced growth hormone production (impairs recovery)

When sleep quality tendency is **good**, the body naturally enters and maintains deep, restorative sleep phases.
