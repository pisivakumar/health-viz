# Fat Metabolism

How well your body breaks down and uses dietary fats. Good fat metabolism supports heart health, brain function, and hormone production.

## Derivation Rules

- **Low**: Triglycerides above optimal OR HDL below optimal (significant deviation)
- **Moderate**: Triglycerides or HDL near boundary
- **High**: All lipid markers (Triglycerides, HDL, LDL) within optimal range

## What This Means for the User

When fat metabolism is **low**, the user may experience:
- Elevated cardiovascular risk markers
- Poor absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Suboptimal hormone production (hormones are built from cholesterol)
- Difficulty with sustained energy between meals

When fat metabolism is **high**, fats are efficiently converted to energy and properly distributed.

## Talking Points

- "Your lipid panel suggests your body is processing fats well" (if high)
- "Omega-3 fatty acids are one of the most well-studied supports for lipid optimization"
- "Reducing refined carbohydrates often has a bigger impact on triglycerides than reducing fat intake"
- "Zone 2 cardio (brisk walking, easy cycling) is excellent for improving fat metabolism"
