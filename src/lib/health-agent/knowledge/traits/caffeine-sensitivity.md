# Caffeine Sensitivity

COMT gene variants slow the breakdown of catecholamines (dopamine, adrenaline). Slower COMT means caffeine lingers longer, amplifying its stimulant effects.

## Derivation Rules

- **High**: COMT enzyme activity is "significantly-reduced" OR Cortisol above optimal range
- **Moderate**: COMT enzyme activity is "reduced" OR Cortisol near upper optimal boundary
- **Low**: COMT enzyme activity is "normal" AND Cortisol within optimal range

## What This Means for the User

When caffeine sensitivity is **high**, the user may experience:
- Jitteriness or anxiety from small amounts of caffeine
- Difficulty sleeping even if caffeine was consumed early in the day
- Elevated cortisol that compounds stress

When caffeine sensitivity is **low**, caffeine is processed efficiently and has minimal negative impact on sleep or stress hormones.

## Talking Points

- "Your COMT gene suggests caffeine may linger in your system longer than average"
- "This doesn't mean you can't have caffeine — just that timing and amount matter more for you"
- "Caffeine after noon may be affecting your sleep quality more than you realize"
