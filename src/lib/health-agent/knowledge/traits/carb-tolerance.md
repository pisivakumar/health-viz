# Carb Tolerance

How efficiently your body processes carbohydrates into energy. Low tolerance means blood sugar spikes more easily, and excess carbs are stored as fat.

## Derivation Rules

- **Low**: HbA1c OR Glucose OR Insulin above optimal range (2+ markers out)
- **Moderate**: One marker near the upper boundary
- **High**: All three markers within optimal range

## What This Means for the User

When carb tolerance is **low**, the user may experience:
- Energy crashes 2-3 hours after carb-heavy meals
- Afternoon fatigue and brain fog
- Difficulty losing weight despite calorie restriction
- Higher fasting glucose trending toward insulin resistance

When carb tolerance is **high**, the body handles carbohydrates efficiently with stable blood sugar.

## Talking Points

- "Your blood sugar markers suggest your body may be working harder to process carbohydrates"
- "Eating protein and fat before carbs at each meal can blunt the glucose spike"
- "A 10-15 minute walk after meals is one of the simplest ways to support blood sugar"
- "This isn't about avoiding carbs entirely — it's about choosing the right ones and the right timing"
